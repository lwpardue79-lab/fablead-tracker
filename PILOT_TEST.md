# FabLead Tracker: 1-Week Company Pilot

Use this guide to test FabLead Tracker with one fabrication or welding company in the Kansas City metro.

## Ideal pilot company

Pick a shop that:

- does structural steel, miscellaneous metals, rails, stairs, platforms, repair welding, or custom fabrication;
- wants more commercial, public, industrial, or GC bid invites;
- has one owner, estimator, or office manager willing to spend 20 minutes per day for one week;
- already has a capability statement, website, or basic company profile.

## What you are testing

The app is not being tested as “a CRM.” The core question is:

> Does FabLead help a fab shop find real buyers, understand how to get on bid lists, take action, and remember follow-ups?

## Pilot setup

1. Open the app at `http://127.0.0.1:3000`.
2. Go to **Pilot Test**.
3. Enter the shop name, tester name, primary work type, and service radius.
4. Go to **Companies** and review the Kansas City buyer directory.
5. Mark which buyers are useful, missing, already known, or not a fit.
6. Use the buyer detail pages to open registration/prequalification links.
7. Track each action in **Follow-Ups** or the Pilot checklist.

## Suggested one-week schedule

### Day 1 — Fit check

- Review all buyer records.
- Ask whether each one is a real potential buyer for the shop.
- Capture missing buyer types or trade scopes.

### Day 2 — Registration paths

- Start at least three bid-list or vendor-registration paths.
- Record whether each one is clear, confusing, or too burdensome.

### Day 3 — Outreach

- Send 5 intro emails or make 5 calls.
- Use the shop’s capability statement.
- Log any replies, bounce-backs, or referrals.

### Day 4 — Follow-up discipline

- Check whether FabLead makes follow-ups obvious.
- Add the next step for every buyer contacted.

### Day 5 — Owner review

- Export the pilot report from **Pilot Test**.
- Ask whether the shop would pay for:
  - a refreshed Kansas City package;
  - a state-by-state package;
  - monthly buyer and bid updates;
  - done-for-you setup and outreach tracking.

## Success criteria

Consider the pilot promising if:

- 3+ buyer records cause a concrete action;
- the shop says the data saved research time;
- at least one registration, intro email, or call happens during the pilot;
- the owner can name a price they would pay;
- missing data is specific enough to improve the next version.

Do not overbuild CRM features until buyer data quality is proven.

## Questions to ask the shop

1. Which buyers were most useful?
2. Which buyers were missing?
3. Which trade scopes should FabLead filter by?
4. Did the bid-list instructions make sense?
5. What information was missing before you could act?
6. Would you pay for this as software, a regional lead package, or a done-for-you setup service?
7. How often would the data need to be refreshed?

## Recommended paid pilot offer

Start simple:

- **$250–$500 one-time setup** for one shop
- Includes KC buyer package, onboarding, capability-statement review, and first-week follow-up setup
- Optional monthly refresh later at **$49–$149/month**

The early paid version should be sold as business-development data plus organization, not as a full CRM replacement.
